# JobHunt go-to-market playbook

Synthesized from channel research (X, LinkedIn, India communities, launch platforms), tailored to:
a **free**, **anti-spam**, **break-even** job-matcher for **early-career Indian developers**, run by a
**solo founder**. This is an honest playbook, not a hype deck.

## The one thing to internalize first

Growth here is **word-of-mouth from genuinely delighted users + steady presence in the communities
where Indian freshers actually are.** It is NOT launch-day virality, not Product Hunt, not an
automated bot. Every channel below is a slow compounding game (60-90 days before it pays). Anyone
promising a hockey stick is selling something.

**Hard prerequisite (don't market before this):** prove the product is genuinely good with your
current users (the feedback loop / "I couldn't have found this myself" bar). Marketing a tool people
don't love just burns the channels. Get a few friends saying "this is genuinely useful" first , their
words become your best marketing copy.

## Before you post anywhere (the setup)

1. **Value visible WITHOUT signup.** A public demo / sample matches / a screenshot of a real Telegram
   digest. A signup wall kills every Reddit/community post.
2. **UTM tags per channel** (`?utm_source=reddit` etc.) so you measure which channel actually *retains*
   users, not just spikes.
3. **A one-line value prop**, leading with the pain, not the tech: *"Personalized fresh jobs from 150+
   company career pages, to your Telegram, free, no spam."*
4. **One build-story article** drafted once, reused everywhere: *"How I built a job-matcher that
   scrapes 150+ career pages and matches them to your resume."* (The engineering angle is your HN/blog
   hook; the pain angle is your Reddit/LinkedIn hook.)
5. A complete **Peerlist** profile.

## Your unfair advantage as content (use everywhere)

You scrape 150+ career pages daily. **That output IS your best marketing.** "Free list" posts convert
because you give the value *before* asking:
> "7 companies quietly hiring 2024/25 grads this week (direct apply links) , I send these matched to
> your resume, daily, free. 👇"
Use this format on Reddit, LinkedIn, and X. It's relatable, useful, and impossible to call spam.

## Per-channel quick playbook

**Reddit (r/developersIndia, r/cscareerquestionsIndia):**
- Spend 2-3 weeks genuinely answering fresher job questions first (build karma; new accounts dropping
  links get auto-removed).
- Then ONE build-in-public post: "I got tired of refreshing 150 career pages, so I built a free tool
  that DMs me only the jobs matching my resume , here's how it works." Screenshots, the matching
  logic, the tracker. **No link in the title. No signup wall. Live in the comments for days.**
- Read each sub's live sidebar rules before posting (90/10 self-promo norm; mods are strict).
- Never cross-post the identical post to 5 subs same day (= ban).

**Peerlist Launchpad:** Prep cover image + tagline, launch Monday, reply to every comment all day,
post your maker story. Promote only to people who already know you (no cold upvote-begging , it's
against the rules and the culture).

**LinkedIn (personal profile, never a company page):**
- Carousels are your highest-leverage format ("X freshers-hiring companies this week", "ATS resume
  mistakes"). 3-4 posts/week.
- Post self-contained value; put the link in the **first comment + your profile**, not the body
  (link-in-body cuts reach ~30-40%).
- Warm your network 2-3 weeks before any ask; personal DMs to 20-30 genuine supporters beat public
  posts ~10x. Skip LinkedIn Groups (dead/spam).

**X/Twitter:**
- Growth at 0 followers = **30-50 genuine replies/day** to Indian dev/placement accounts, NOT posting
  into the void. Consider X Premium (10x reach + pinned replies) , cheap, highest single lever.
- 1 post/day, evening IST, reply to all comments within the hour (first-hour velocity = reach).
- Link in bio + first reply, never the main post. Pitch ~1 in 8 replies; give value the other 7.

**College clubs:** Cold-email coding-club / GDSC leads (easier than TPOs), recipient-first: "helping
your final-year students catch off-campus drives they currently miss," offer a free-for-your-batch
link or a short club talk. One yes = 100-500 right-fit freshers.

**Show HN:** One shot, framed as the engineering story ("Show HN: I scrape 150+ career pages and match
jobs to your resume"). Tue-Thu morning ET, be present for comments. Don't expect Indian-fresher users
from it , it's for credibility + a backlink.

## Universal anti-spam rules (your wedge is anti-spam , don't violate it)

- Give value 5-10x before any link, in every community.
- Never the same promo across multiple places same day.
- No templated/copy-paste replies, no follow/unfollow, no bought followers, no auto-DM tools.
- Never drop links in Telegram/WhatsApp job channels (you're not the admin; it's spam or paid ads on a
  competitor's channel). These are **data sources** for you, not distribution.
- Always have one clear path to signup; reach without a CTA is vanity.

## 4-week launch sequence

- **Week 0 (setup):** public demo, UTM tags, value prop, build-story article, Peerlist profile. Start
  earning Reddit karma + commenting on LinkedIn/X in your niche. No promo yet.
- **Week 1 (content/SEO):** publish the build-story article on dev.to + Hashnode (cross-post).
- **Week 2 (right-audience launch):** Peerlist Launchpad (Monday) + a genuine story post on
  r/developersIndia. Engage all day/week.
- **Week 3 (credibility):** Show HN (engineering framing). Start cold-emailing college club leads.
  First LinkedIn "free list" carousel + first X "free list" thread.
- **Week 4 (compound):** "Month 1: what happened" transparency post (LinkedIn + X + Indie Hackers).
  Double down on whatever format/channel converted. Set the sustainable rhythm (LinkedIn 3x/week, X
  daily replies, Reddit value comments).

## Skip / waste of time
- **Product Hunt** , wrong audience (US/SaaS), 50-120hr cost. Maybe never, definitely not now.
- **BetaList** , pre-launch only; you're already live.
- **LinkedIn Groups, r/IMadeThis, Slack senior-dev communities** , dead or wrong audience.
- **An automated marketing/sales bot** , the spam that kills products like this.

## Measure
Track **signups + retention per channel** (UTM), and the quality metric (do users apply / find jobs
they couldn't have themselves). Optimize for those, not followers/upvotes/impressions.
